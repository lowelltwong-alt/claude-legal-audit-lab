# Decision record: defensive ontology-capture counterfactual

## Decision and risk tier

**Risk tier: high.** The work changes shared schemas, registries, navigation,
threat-model language, and future-agent defaults. A poor design could either
publish an extraction blueprint or create false confidence that metadata is
harmless.

The repository owner explicitly requested a counterfactual lane and routed
subagents. That authorizes a candidate public/synthetic build, not release,
real-data use, a provider accusation, a legal conclusion, or a live collection
system. Named human release approval remains required.

## Options considered

### A. Narrative only

Lowest implementation risk and easiest to read, but hard to validate, difficult
to connect across a 23-stage lifecycle, and easy for future agents to omit
limitations or controls.

### B. Typed exposure-and-controls graph — selected

Authored registries define stages and counterfactual patterns. A deterministic,
offline builder connects stages, artifacts, signals, possible inferences,
limitations, controls, and public sources. It is expressive enough for research
while making prohibited crossings testable.

### C. Live telemetry or elicitation harness — rejected

This would create unnecessary privacy, privilege, consent, security, and abuse
risk. It could be repurposed for covert collection or workforce monitoring. No
endpoint, exporter, credential access, real-user input, or collection flag is
included.

## Architecture constitution

1. Public or conspicuously fictional sources only.
2. No network, telemetry SDK, credentials, home-directory, clipboard, browser,
   email, client, matter, or production-log access.
3. Processing, retention, telemetry, feedback, secondary use, and training are
   separate propositions.
4. Confidentiality is broader than privilege; privilege, work product, privacy,
   and trade secrets remain separate.
5. Every pattern carries applicability, non-applicability, danger,
   countercontrols, and does-not-prove statements.
6. Public-record reconstruction must preserve sealed, unfiled, confidential,
   internal-deliberation, and selection-bias gaps.
7. Acquisition remains a jurisdiction-specific governance scenario with no
   evasion or transaction instructions.
8. Generated graphs are candidate evidence and cannot promote themselves.

## Premortem

| Failure | Early signal | Control |
|---|---|---|
| Defensive model becomes an extraction recipe | Manipulative prompt text, outbound endpoint, collection flag, or real log reader appears | Static and behavioral tests; human review; kill the lane |
| Token metadata is overstated | A claim maps counts directly to a legal position | Required does-not-prove edges and explicit weak-semantic boundary |
| Current terms are ignored | Processing is described as training or optional feedback as default product use | Dated provider sources and separate proposition fields |
| Public filings are treated as full strategy | Internal rationale appears without a source or uncertainty edge | Public-record pattern requires incompleteness and internal-deliberation exclusion |
| "Buy a firm" becomes evasion advice | A structure is proposed to bypass Rule 5.4, privilege, conflicts, or UPL | Contract/policy-review-only mode; no transaction code; jurisdiction gate |
| Synthetic fixtures are copied from real matters | Unique dates, parties, amounts, or docket identifiers appear | No real-world identifiers; manual release review; fixture provenance |
| Generated graph becomes source truth | A derived edge is cited without source closure | Candidate-evidence status and public lineage validator |

## Independent review findings incorporated

- Operational topology is often inferable without content, but attorney
  judgment needs context–choice–rationale–outcome links.
- Token counts and latency are weak alone and become sensitive through joins.
- Information relating to a representation is broader than privilege.
- Intake and conflicts require protection before or apart from a completed
  engagement.
- Formal Opinion 512 and current provider terms constrain overclaims but do not
  prove runtime architecture.
- Public records cannot recover sealed, unfiled, confidential, or internal work.
- Ownership regimes vary; acquisition does not imply client-data authority.

## Rollback and kill criteria

Rollback is deletion of the new counterfactual registries, schemas, builder,
generated graph, prompt, template, tests, decision record, and navigation and
lineage rows. Existing evidence and private research remain untouched.

Immediately stop and require a named human decision if any change:

- enables live collection, networking, identity linkage, cross-customer linkage,
  training, secondary use, or production-data reads;
- requests real client, prospective-client, conflicts, settlement, billing,
  trust, or attorney-performance data;
- adds deceptive prompts or regulatory-evasion instructions;
- weakens the public/private boundary;
- claims actual provider conduct or intent without exact evidence; or
- moves from candidate research to public release or real-firm use.

Structured safety fields control over conflicting prose. The validator rejects
missing contextual sources, disabled authority outside the public-source-only
lane, child-edge source inheritance, sealed or restricted public-record inputs,
internal-deliberation inference, acquisition evasion design, transaction
playbooks, or removal of jurisdiction-specific review.

## Validation plan

The builder must validate the fixed safety policy, all 23 stages, source and
graph references, synthetic-only treatment of content-bearing patterns,
contract-review-only treatment of acquisition, public-source-only treatment of
docket reconstruction, controls, limitations, and deterministic output. The
repository's navigation, lineage, privacy, and unit-test suites must also pass.
