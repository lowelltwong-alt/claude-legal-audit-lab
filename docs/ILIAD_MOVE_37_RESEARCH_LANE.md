# Iliad Line 37 and Move 37 Research Lane

This is a candidate white-paper research lane. It explores whether the Iliad's
Trojan-War imagery and AlphaGo's Move 37 can clarify a *counterfactual* concern:
how a legal-AI system might become strategically valuable if a firm exposes its
workflow ontology and collective professional judgment. It does not allege that
any provider has captured, trained on, or intends to capture a firm's ontology.

## Research question

Can a law firm preserve workflow and attorney-brain-trust sovereignty while
using AI systems, and what evidence would distinguish local assistance, agreed
data processing, authorized model improvement, and prohibited or unproven
secondary use?

## Evidence requirements

1. Verify each proposed Iliad line against an identified critical edition and
   licensed translation; book/line numbering must be recorded exactly.
2. Use `SRC-0042` through `SRC-0045` for the bounded AlphaGo/algorithmic
   context. Move 37 is an analogy, not a prediction of legal competence.
3. Use feature-specific contracts, retention documents, connector documentation,
   and pinned repository evidence for legal-AI statements.
4. Preserve counterevidence: customer terms, local-only configuration, human
   review, tenant controls, and the absence of a reproducible source-to-sink
   path each limit the hypothesis.

## White-paper rule

The public paper may say that non-obvious strategy is a research concern. It
may not say that a legal AI has acquired attorney creativity, extracted a law
firm's brain trust, or acted as a Trojan Horse unless the specific proposition
has exact, reproducible evidence and its counterevidence is shown alongside it.

## Trojan-War governance lens

The private working map uses Paris, the Judgment of Paris, the golden apple,
Helen, the walls of Troy, Chryses' appeal, and Apollo only as analytical
metaphors for decision-making, valued assets, boundaries, triggers, and remote
dependencies. Public work must not identify a real person or organization with
one of those roles, and must not treat metaphor as proof of deception, capture,
or intent.
