# Questions for Kirkland and Palantir

1. Who owns the ontology, workflow definitions, prompts, evaluation sets, corrections, and execution traces?
2. Which models are used, and where do they run?
3. Do any frontier providers receive full partner playbooks or only minimized task fragments?
4. What does “Palantir will not access confidential client data” mean technically and contractually?
5. Can Palantir access non-client workflow artifacts, partner rationales, model evaluations, or aggregate telemetry?
6. Is the control plane model-neutral, and has Kirkland tested model replacement?
7. Are partner corrections and outcome data retained solely in Kirkland-controlled systems?
8. Can the complete system be exported and operated without Palantir?
9. How are conflicts, ethical walls, matter permissions, privilege, and jurisdictional residency enforced?
10. Which artifacts are deliberately excluded from any frontier-model prompt?
11. What contractual limits prevent secondary product use or competitive derivation?
12. How will the firm validate that senior-partner judgment is not flattened into generic consensus?
